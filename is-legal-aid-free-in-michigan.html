FAQ

What is legal aid?

Legal aid refers to the provision of free or low-cost legal assistance to individuals who cannot afford the services of a lawyer.

Is legal aid free in Michigan?

While legal aid services are available in Michigan, they may not always be completely free. The cost of legal aid can vary depending on factors such as income level and the type of legal assistance needed.

Who is eligible for legal aid in Michigan?

Eligibility for legal aid in Michigan is typically based on income level. Individuals with low income, limited assets, and who meet other specific criteria may qualify for legal aid services.

How can I determine if I am eligible for legal aid in Michigan?

To determine your eligibility for legal aid in Michigan, you can contact your local legal aid organization or visit their website. They will have specific guidelines and requirements that you can review to see if you qualify for their services.

What types of legal issues does legal aid cover in Michigan?

Legal aid in Michigan can cover a wide range of civil legal issues, such as landlord-tenant disputes, family law matters, consumer protection, public benefits, and more. Each legal aid organization may have specific areas of focus, so it's best to check with them directly.

Yes, there may be alternatives available for individuals who do not qualify for legal aid or cannot access it.
